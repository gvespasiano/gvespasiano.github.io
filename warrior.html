Can Ice Cream be a Health Food?


Warrior is a brand of ice cream that focuses on creating healthy products to go along with an active lifestyle. For this project, I created a set of strategic brand guidelines for the company and then applied them to packaging, advertising, website designs, and social media assets.







Brand Design

When creating the visual identity for the brand I was struck by a passage I covered in a graphic design course that stated "...instead of focusing on the graphic design object, [focus] on designing systems and methodologies that lead to design... we must shift our focus from the physicality of the objects that are produced (the surface) to the research that leads to the products (the process)." I wanted to design a system that was based on background research to help their product stand out from their competition. By studying existing brands and developing an identity for the product, I was able to ground the brand with a visual system that lends itself to assets from packaging to web design.



Finding Category White Space

In order to create a brand that would stand out in a saturated market, I first began by looking at our competitors. From these competing brands I was then able to define a list of clichés and potential white space for my brand to fill.



Clichés

In order to create a brand that would stand out in a saturated market, I first began by looking at our competitors. From these competing brands I was then able to define a list of clichés and potential white space for my brand to fill.

  • Enticing Flavors
  • Decadence
  • Ingredients Shown Alongside the Ice Cream
  • Pastel Colors
  • Simple Patterns
  • Individually Packaing Each Flavor
  • Avoiding Guilt
White Space

I wanted the Warrior brand to be strong enough to not only lean on the motif of the active lifestyle. By identifying blue ocean for my product to live in, I was able to better set the brand apart.

  • Active Imagery
  • Consistent Packaging
  • Focus on Fuel Over Flavor
  • Brand as Part of a Nutrition Routine
  • Less Focus on the Product, More Focus on the Athlete
  • Owning One Color


Brand Archetype

To help structure my brand guidelines, I chose a brand archetype that would further help my products stand out. Based on Carl Jung's 12 Brand Archetypes, I chose the "rebel" to guide my design and to create Warrior's unique identity. The rebel is described as "Rebellious, iconoclastic, wild, paving the way for change". The main goal of rebranding ice cream as a health food, requires a change of mindset in how I'd present my brand and this archetype is perfect for shaking things up. Along with my brand white space, I also sought to determine "what does a "rebel" look like in the ice cream category?".

Web Design

My design for Warrior's website focuses on building community, and helping users find where to purchase the product. The design maintains a consistent brand identity that promotes the product as a part of a healthy lifestyle. Unlike a traditional e-commerce site, our product can't be sold over the internet so I designed the page to drive users into the site's store locator function. In order to help them capture traffic for further marketing efforts, the site integrates a content marketing strategy that allows users to sign up for daily updates in allignment with the overall brand.
