Rethinking How We Share Content


MileStones is a decentralized social media platform that allows you to safely and conveniently share your family's big moments. The project started as an assignment to create and photo sharing application for new parents. After interviewing several potential users I found a set of desired features that are not being met by social media and designed an application to better serve this set of users.

The application allows users to set up secure timelines that they fill with their lives’ moments and then share directly with their friends and family. By stripping away much of the connectivity and intrusiveness of traditional social media platforms, users have more control over the content they share, and who has access to it.








Background Research

User Interviews

To help me understand the specific needs of this unique set of users, I began my background research with a round of interviews. These interviews focused on how they were currently using social media, how they'd ideally like to share their family's content, and how their family might like to recieve this content. I interviewed three users on the below questions and used their responses to help guide my product requirements.

  • Think of the first year after you had your child. What is the most important thing you wanted to share with your friends and family?
  • Given current methods of photo and video sharing, do you feel secure that your family photos will remain private?
  • How often do you check social media? What about email?
  • Think of your extended family, what formats might your grandparents or another older family member find the easiest to view?
  • If you were to receive updates on another family’s milestones, how often would you want to receive them? What would be your preferred method?


Competetive Analysis

After interviewing potential users on their expectations and desires from my product, I then began looking at the technologies that would shape their interactions with MileStones. I began my research with some of the social media heavy hitters before diving into differing technologies with parallel functions that users would have been pre-trained to understand. In combination with my user interviews, this helped inform my set of required features and the expectations from my user personas.

User Personas

To help me empathise with the needs of potential users, I created two personas based on my interview findings. I created seperate user personas as there are two very different groups of people who will be using my product, and taking the time to map out their needs, frustrations, and tasks helped me build a product that would work for both sets of users. Persona #1 represents the new parents who will be creating the content timelines, and persona #2 shows the users that will be viewing the content posted on these timelines.

Product Requirements

With the data from my background research, I developed a set of product requirements that were necessary for my personas succesfully use my product. From these requirements, I designed two flows that I would create create for my initial round of testing.

Flow #1 details the onboarding process for new "content creator" users. Here they are taken through the steps of setting up their timeline preferances, adding/managing their subscribers, and then posting content.

Flow #2 shows how my "content consumer" persona would be able to interact with the application. Their journey begins with an initial invitation email, a browser visit to a specific MileStones URL, or a piece of content from a timeline they are subscribed to. From here they are then able to view the timeline as well as manage their subscription preferances. The flow I will design in my prototype is highlighted in green.

Brand Guidelines

To guide the look and feel of my product, I began by creating a set of brand guidelines. In my guidelines, I created an initial brand archetype that would define the visual tone of the application including typography, image usage, and colors. I then built an atomic UI system and examples for responsive designs that would allow for future designers and developers to quickly work within my style guide.



ssssBrand Archetype: The Caregiver

What makes milestones different from other social media platforms? I began developing a style for my product by focusing on the values that make it different. These values were: privacy, family, safety.

In order to create a design that visually conveyed these values, I looked for a brand archetype that I could use as a model. The Caregiver derives meaning from helping others. This brand archetype is moved by compassion and generosity, and strives to make people feel nurtured and secure.

Above all else, the Ui for MileStones should visually make the user feel: Loved, taken care of, safe, “I’m not alone in this,” secure, and thankful .



ssssImage Guidelines

First and foremost, images should focus on families and children. The images should include diverse families and, as much as possible, evoke happiness.

Non user-generated images will appear in black and white, or with the purple (#400057) tint applied.

On mobile screens, images should span the length from logo to menu.
