About Me

As a designer, I like to look at everyday inconveniences as opportunities to create something that makes life easier. With the ubiquity of mobile technology, these opportunities for improvement are everywhere and can benefit consumers and businesses alike.





Minneapolis College of Art and Design

MA - Graphic & Web Design

This past semester I completed my Masters of Arts in Graphic & Web Design. During my coursework, I sharpened my design principles while learning new techniques and software. While completing the program I completed project work for print, web, digital marketing, and mobile applications. For my capstone project, I designed a mobile application for the Minnesota Twins’ baseball stadium, Target Field.

Minneapolis College of Art and Design

Certificate - User Experience Design

After taking my first UX course in the MA program, I wanted to continue learning about the process of planning and designing mobile and web applications. The certificate program includes four ten week courses that teach user experience design, user research, user interface design, and prototyping. In these courses, I produced several application prototypes as well as participated in a group heuristic analysis of a non-profit organization’s website.

University of Wisconsin - Madison

BA - Art History & Legal Studies

While in college, I double majored in Art History and Legal Studies. Art history taught me creative communication through the study of art, philosophy and history. Being able to write compelling and clear arguments about a painting or a sculpture is a skill that has proved invaluable in my approach to marketing frameworks. My work in legal Studies involved a more analytical communication style with the combination of history, law, and sociology. This taught me strategic and logical communication skills to complement my work in Art History.
